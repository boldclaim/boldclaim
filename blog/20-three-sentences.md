# Three Sentences That End Any AfD Conversation

Most people don't want a 45-minute briefing on the AfD. They want one thing they can say when someone at work or at dinner drops "but the AfD is the only party that speaks the truth." Something short, factual, and hard to argue with.

Here are three sentences. Each one is a standalone killer fact. Each one comes from an authority that cannot be dismissed as left-wing media. You don't need all three. Any one of them is enough to shift the conversation. Together, they're unanswerable.

## Sentence One: The Voting Record

"The AfD voted against the minimum wage increase, against the Kindergeld increase, and against the Mietpreisbremse in the Bundestag. In ten years and 152 seats, they haven't passed a single law."

**Why it works:** This is the universal banger. It works on conservatives, on the undecided, on ex-AfD voters, on young people. It works because almost nobody checks parliamentary voting records, and the AfD's actual record contradicts everything they claim to stand for. The party that says it fights for the "kleiner Mann" (ordinary working person) voted against every policy that directly helps ordinary working people.

**Source:** Bundestag voting protocols (bundestag.de), Rosa-Luxemburg-Stiftung analysis of AfD voting behaviour, DGB (German Trade Union Federation) analysis of the 2025 election programme.

**When they say** "but they're fighting the system!": Voting against the minimum wage is not fighting the system. It's fighting the people who earn it. Fighting the system would mean proposing better alternatives. The AfD proposed nothing. Zero legislative achievements in a decade.

## Sentence Two: The Conviction

"The AfD's parliamentary leader in Thuringia was convicted twice for using an SA slogan, both convictions upheld by the highest criminal court in Germany, and he's still in his job."

**Why it works:** This is not an accusation. It's not an opinion. It's a court ruling. Bjoern Hoecke, a trained history teacher, used the SA (Nazi paramilitary) slogan "Alles fuer Deutschland" at two campaign events. He was convicted by the Halle regional court in May 2024 and again in July 2024. The Bundesgerichtshof (Federal Court of Justice, Germany's highest criminal court) upheld both convictions in August 2025. The AfD drew zero consequences. He remains parliamentary group leader.

**Source:** BGH press release, August 2025. ZDF reporting. LTO (Legal Tribune Online).

**When they say** "he didn't know it was an SA slogan!": He is a history teacher. The BGH explicitly ruled that he used the slogan knowingly. He appealed twice. He lost twice. The highest court in the country says he knew. That settles it.

**When they say** "it's just a phrase!": It is a phrase that was the official motto of the SA, the organisation that beat, tortured, and murdered political opponents, Jewish citizens, and anyone who got in their way. German law prohibits it for a reason. A court confirmed the prohibition applies. Twice.

**When they say** "what about the party, not just one person?": That's exactly the point. The party kept him. A convicted user of Nazi slogans remains their parliamentary leader in Thuringia. This is not a rogue individual. This is a deliberate institutional choice.

## Sentence Three: The Price Tag

"The Institut der deutschen Wirtschaft (the employers' think tank, not a left-wing organisation) calculated that AfD economic policy would cost Germany 690 billion euros and 2.5 million jobs within five years."

**Why it works:** The source is what makes this sentence lethal. The IW Koeln is close to the BDI (Federation of German Industries) and the BDA (Confederation of German Employers' Associations). It is nobody's idea of a left-wing outfit. When the employers' own think tank says your economic programme is a disaster, the "but the left-wing media is biased" defence collapses.

The 690 billion figure comes from modelling the AfD's stated goal of leaving the EU (which they euphemistically call "overcoming" the EU). Over 40% of German economic output comes from exports, and more than half of those exports go to EU countries. A Dexit is not a political statement. It's an economic suicide note.

**Source:** IW Koeln study on Dexit costs. DIW Berlin (German Institute for Economic Research) assessment: "AfD economic policy leads to catastrophe." Familienunternehmer-Verband (Family Business Association) analysis paper: "The economically hostile policies of the AfD."

**When they say** "those are just predictions!": Based on the same economic modelling that predicted the costs of Brexit (which turned out to be roughly accurate). The IW, DIW, and Handelsblatt are not in the business of political activism. They count money.

## How to Use Them

You don't need all three. Pick the one that fits your audience.

Talking to someone who cares about money? Sentence three. Talking to someone who thinks the AfD is a protest party? Sentence one. Talking to someone who says "they're not that extreme"? Sentence two.

Deliver it calmly. One sentence, one source, one question: "Did you know that?" Then stop talking. Let the silence work. Don't pile on. Don't lecture. The fact is the payload. Your job is just to deliver it and step back.

If they dismiss it, repeat the source. "That's not my opinion. That's the Bundesgerichtshof / the Bundestag voting record / the employers' own think tank." Authorities are harder to dismiss than arguments. That's why every sentence comes with one.

And if they still dismiss it? That's fine. You weren't talking to them. You were talking to the person at the table who was listening quietly and just learned something they didn't know before.
