# 200 vs 5

Over 200 people have been murdered by right-wing extremists in Germany since 1990. The number from the left: under 5. That single comparison demolishes the "both sides" argument more effectively than any essay could. But the full picture is worse.

## The government's own numbers

The BKA's politically motivated crime statistics for 2024 set a new record. Not a modest increase. An explosion.

Right-wing politically motivated offences: 42,788. That is more than half of all politically motivated crime in Germany. Up 47.8% from the previous year. Left-wing offences: 9,971. Up 28%, but starting from a base 4.3 times smaller.

Right-wing violent crimes: approximately 1,500, up 17.2%. Left-wing violent crimes: 762, down 17%. Read that again. Right-wing violence rose by the same percentage that left-wing violence fell. The trends are moving in opposite directions.

BKA President Holger Muench called the 2024 figures the highest since recording began in 2001. Interior Minister Dobrindt (CSU, not exactly a left-wing firebrand) confirmed: the greatest threat comes from right-wing extremism. His predecessor Seehofer, also CSU, said the same thing. Seehofer's predecessor Faeser (SPD) said the same thing. Every interior minister for the past decade, regardless of party, has reached the same conclusion.

## The body count

The Amadeu Antonio Foundation documents 221 victims of right-wing violence since German reunification. The NSU murdered ten people over years while the domestic intelligence service looked the other way. The Halle synagogue attack. The Hanau massacre. The assassination of politician Walter Luebcke. Over 1,000 attacks on refugee shelters since 2015. In 2024 alone, 196 attacks on asylum accommodation, up 32%.

From the left: isolated cases. No serial murders. No terrorist networks. No assassination of elected officials. The typical left-wing offences are property damage, clashes with police at demonstrations, and arson of cars. Ugly, criminal, worth prosecuting. But not comparable to systematic murder.

The difference is not just quantitative. It is qualitative. Right-wing extremists target people (synagogues, mosques, refugee homes, politicians). Left-wing extremists target structures (police cars, corporate offices, construction sites). One side murders. The other vandalises.

## The horseshoe is broken

The horseshoe theory, the idea that the far left and far right curve towards each other and are essentially mirror images, remains popular because it sounds balanced. Reasonable, even. "Both extremes are bad" requires no thought and offends nobody in the centre.

The 2024 Verfassungsschutzbericht dismantles it with numbers. Right-wing extremist persons of interest: 50,250, up 23.8% in a single year. Left-wing: 38,000, up 2.7%. Violence-oriented individuals on the right: 15,300. On the left: 11,200. The right is not only larger but growing faster, by an order of magnitude.

Two-thirds of all hate crime in Germany falls under right-wing politically motivated crime. Anti-woman offences rose 73.3%, with more than half right-wing motivated. The number of juvenile suspects (14-17) in right-wing offences more than doubled year on year, 89% of them boys.

Left-wing violence against police, often cited as the great left-wing menace, fell by 51.4%. Halved in a single year.

## Who benefits from "both sides"

The false equivalence serves one side only. If both extremes are equally dangerous, then there is no particular reason to focus on the right. The AfD gets to present itself as unfairly targeted. The conversation shifts from "right-wing extremism is the dominant domestic threat" to "all extremism is bad", which sounds identical but means something completely different in practice. It diffuses urgency. It distributes blame. It protects the side that is actually killing people.

20,000 AfD members were counted as part of the right-wing extremist persons of interest in 2024. That is nearly 40% of the entire right-wing extremist potential in Germany. The Verfassungsschutz calls the AfD "the defining actor within the right-wing extremist party spectrum." This is not an opposition talking point. It is the assessment of the domestic intelligence service, confirmed by independent administrative courts, under a CSU interior minister.

## The numbers do not require interpretation

42,788 vs 9,971. Over 200 dead vs under 5. Right-wing violence rising 17% while left-wing violence drops 17%. These are not opinions. They are the federal government's own statistics, compiled by the BKA and the Verfassungsschutz, presented by a CSU interior minister, and confirmed by every predecessor in the role.

"Both sides" is not neutrality. It is innumeracy.
