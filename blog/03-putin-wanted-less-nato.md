# Putin Wanted Less NATO

In February 2022, Putin invaded Ukraine with four objectives: topple Zelensky, destroy Ukraine as a sovereign state, push NATO back from Russia's borders, and reassert Russia as a great power. Three years later, every single objective has failed. The balance sheet is not close.

## The strategic ledger

**What Putin wanted vs what he got:**

NATO membership before the invasion: 30 countries. After: 32. Finland, neutral since 1949, joined in April 2023. Sweden, neutral for over 200 years, joined in March 2024. Finland's accession alone added 1,340 km of new NATO border with Russia, more than doubling the previous 1,215 km. Putin wanted less NATO on his borders. He got twice as much.

Zelensky was supposed to flee Kyiv in the first 72 hours. He stayed. He is still there. Ukraine exists as a sovereign, internationally recognised state with deeper Western integration than at any point in its history.

Russia's share of EU gas imports: 40% in 2021. By 2025: 12%. The EU Council voted in January 2026 to ban Russian gas imports entirely, with a deadline of end 2027. Nord Stream is destroyed. The Ukraine gas transit expired on 1 January 2025 and was not renewed. Only TurkStream still operates, and its volumes fell 16% in the first half of 2025. Russia now sells its oil to China at discounts of up to 27 USD per barrel below Brent. China saved an estimated 16 billion USD on cheap Russian oil between 2022 and 2024. Russia went from energy superpower to China's discount petrol station.

## The human cost

CSIS estimates total Russian military losses at approximately 1.2 million (killed, wounded, missing). The UK Ministry of Defence puts the figure around 1 million. Mediazona and BBC Russian, who verify each death by name, have confirmed over 152,000 killed. That is the floor, not the ceiling.

For context: this is more military losses than any major power has suffered since the Second World War. It is five times the total killed in all Russian and Soviet conflicts since 1945 combined. The Soviet-Afghan War (1979-89), which helped bring down the USSR, cost approximately 15,000 lives. Ukraine has cost twenty times that.

Material losses: over 3,000 tanks destroyed (more than Russia's entire pre-war active tank fleet), 10,000+ armoured vehicles, roughly 250 aircraft and helicopters.

In 2025 alone, Russia suffered approximately 415,000 casualties, about 35,000 per month. The army consumes between 10,000 and 30,000 workers from the civilian labour market every month. Military salaries exceed civilian wages by 500+ USD, meaning private industry cannot compete for labour.

## The economy eating itself

Russia's GDP grew 4.1% in 2023-2024. This sounds like resilience until you look at what drove it. Approximately 40% of the federal budget goes to defence. Defence spending reached 6.3-7.3% of GDP. Building tanks increases GDP. It does not increase prosperity.

By 2025, growth had slowed to 1%. Q1 2025 saw the first quarter-on-quarter contraction since the war began. Inflation exceeds 10%. The central bank rate sits at 21%. Civilian sectors are collapsing: metallurgy down 3.3%, textiles down 2.3%, furniture down 10%, oil refineries down 4.5%. Corporate profits fell 8.3% in the first eight months of 2025. Oil and gas revenues dropped 24%.

The national wealth fund's liquid assets shrank 24% within a year, falling below 39 billion USD. Capital Economics forecasts 0.5% growth for 2026.

## The brain drain

Between 650,000 and 900,000 Russians have left the country since February 2022. Only about 8% have returned (OutRush study, 2024). 54% say they would not return even if the war ended. Roughly 100,000 IT specialists emigrated by end of 2022, representing 11% of all Russian software developers.

Unemployment is at a historic low of 2.3%. This is not a sign of health. It means there is nobody left to hire. The Russian central bank itself calls labour shortage the "main problem of the Russian economy." Patent applications fell 13% in 2022 from the brain drain alone.

## China's junior partner

Russia was supposed to be an equal partner to China. Instead, China buys more than half of Russia's seaborne oil exports and dictates the price. China supplies critical components for Russia's arms industry. Russia has no bargaining position. The "no limits partnership" announced weeks before the invasion has become a supplier-client relationship, with Russia as the supplier of discounted raw materials.

## The balance sheet

Putin wanted Kyiv in three days. He got the longest conventional war in Europe since 1945. He wanted NATO pushed back. He got 1,340 km of new NATO border. He wanted to destroy Ukrainian nationhood. He united it. He wanted energy leverage over Europe. He lost 70% of his gas market share. He wanted to project strength. He lost 1.2 million soldiers, his economy is entering stagflation, and his best engineers have left.

Every line item on the strategic ledger is negative. The war has achieved the opposite of each stated objective. This is not a matter of perspective. It is the kind of failure you can measure in kilometres, dollars, and body bags.
