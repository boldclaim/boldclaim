# The Playbook They Use Against You

I spent six months analysing how right-wing accounts operate on German Reddit. Not the open neo-Nazis (they get banned fast enough). The effective ones. The accounts that shift threads, win lurkers, and make progressive counter-arguments look overwrought. They have patterns, modes, and known weaknesses. Here is the playbook.

## The seven archetypes

Not every right-wing reply is the same. Treating them identically is how you lose threads you should win.

**The Professor.** Long replies. Multiple sources. Structured arguments with strategic concessions built in ("the overrepresentation is real, but..."). They sound authoritative because they often are. Their weakness: they cannot stop writing. A six-paragraph reply to your two-line comment makes them look obsessed. Most readers will not finish it. Their length is their liability.

**The Gotcha Artist.** Waits for you to get one number slightly wrong, then builds the entire reply around the error. Ignores your actual argument. The tell: they quote your exact phrasing, compare it to a source, and declare your credibility destroyed. Their weakness: they are so hungry for the gotcha that they never address the substance. One factual error does real damage to your credibility. But point out that they spent 300 words on a rounding error and zero on the argument, and the lurker notices.

**The Moral Superior.** Does not argue data. Argues values. "This is about human dignity." "This is how it started in the 1930s." The Nazi comparison works specifically on German audiences because nobody wants to be on the wrong side of that analogy. Their weakness: they have made a category error. By turning a factual discussion into a moral one, they have abandoned the terrain where evidence matters. Stay relentlessly factual. The contrast between your calm data and their moral panic reads well for lurkers.

**The Tactic Namer.** The most irritating type. They do not argue your point. They label your technique. "That is a gish gallop." "Classic motte-and-bailey." "You are sealioning." They have read the Wikipedia page on rhetorical fallacies and treat it like a cheat code. Their weakness: naming a tactic is not the same as rebutting an argument. Someone who says "that is a gish gallop" has not actually answered any of your points. Ask a direct, simple question after they apply the label. They either answer (and you are back on your terrain) or dodge (and the lurker sees that the label was the entire act).

**The Source Dumper.** Drops three to five links at the bottom of their reply. Rarely quotes from them. The links do the arguing. Half the time, the sources do not say what the person claims. Their weakness: click the links. About a third of the time the source contradicts their claim, does not support it, or sits behind a paywall they have not read past. If you can say "your second link actually says the opposite of your claim on page 12," they collapse.

**The Bot Accuser.** Dismisses any organised or rapid response as "coordinated," "bot-like," or "astroturfed." If three people disagree with them in the same thread, it must be a brigade. Their weakness: the accusation is unfalsifiable, which also makes it unpersuasive to anyone thinking independently. Someone who says "you are all bots" looks paranoid, not perceptive.

**The Ally Rallier.** Does not argue with you at all. Replies to the person you were debating with "glad you are here" or "do not let them silence you." Reframes the thread from a debate into a solidarity exercise. Makes your factual argument look like harassment. Their weakness: they have abandoned the argument entirely. Ignore them. Engaging makes you look like you are attacking a bystander.

## The four modes

Beyond individual archetypes, these accounts switch between operational modes depending on what serves them.

**Bait Mode.** Post something short, provocative, and slightly wrong. Wait for someone to write 500 words correcting it. The lurker sees a calm two-liner and a progressive who spent their afternoon writing an essay in response. The energy asymmetry is the whole point. Counter-move: match their brevity. Three lines that make them look uninformed. Do not over-invest.

**Flood Mode.** Five comments from five accounts saying variations of the same thing. Quality does not matter. Quantity creates atmosphere. The goal is to make the position look like consensus. Counter-move: recognise it. The talking points will be nearly identical across comments. Point out the pattern once, clearly, then engage the strongest version only.

**Reasonable Mode.** The default and most effective mode. "I am not against immigration, I just think we need to look at the numbers honestly." "I have immigrant friends, but the statistics concern me." Frame the position as the sensible middle ground. Counter-move: do not attack their tone. Engage the substance. If the numbers are wrong, show it. If the framing is misleading, reframe it. The "concern troll" accusation only sticks if their comment history is inconsistent.

**Victim Mode.** Activated when losing on substance. "So I cannot even ask questions anymore?" "Everyone who disagrees with you is a Nazi, right?" "Free speech is dead." The goal is to transform the dynamic from "you said something wrong" to "they are trying to silence dissent." Counter-move: do not give them material. No insults, no condescension, no "educate yourself." Stay flat. Victim mode requires your emotional escalation as fuel. Without it, they have nothing.

## Where they are actually effective (the uncomfortable part)

This is the section most people skip. It is the one that matters.

**They over-invest in individual threads.** They treat every conversation like a battle. They spend 30 minutes on one reply while we spend 30 minutes posting in ten threads. Their quality on any single thread often exceeds ours. A well-sourced, concession-laden reply from the Professor type reads as credible to lurkers who actually finish reading it.

**They are better at sources.** The effective operators read entire studies. They check methodology sections. They know what "controlled for socioeconomic status" means. When they turn your source against you (quoting page 52 after you cited page 51), the lurker sees someone who did the reading and someone who did not. This is a real capability gap.

**They use concessions strategically.** "The overrepresentation is real, but..." buys enormous credibility with undecided readers. The concession makes them look honest. The reframing that follows does the actual work. The counter-move exists (quote the concession, ignore the reframe), but it requires you to notice the structure in real time.

**They stay calm.** Relentlessly. When you escalate, they stay flat. The contrast makes you look unhinged and them look in control. This is not accidental. They have studied how calm responses outperform angry ones with audiences.

**Reasonable mode has no ceiling with the middle.** It wins the moderately concerned lurker every time. Where it fails: the lurker who has done their own research. But most lurkers have not. Most lurkers read tone before they read content. "I am just concerned" is the safest possible positioning and very difficult to attack without looking aggressive.

## What to do with this

Knowing the playbook does not make you immune. It makes you faster at recognising what is happening in a thread before you have already lost it.

Match brevity with brevity. Check their sources (they often have not). Do not give victim mode the emotional fuel it needs. Never let the tactic namer substitute a label for an answer. And when you spot the concession-then-reframe pattern, quote the concession and move on.

The accounts that shift opinions on German Reddit are not loud. They are structured, patient, and methodical. The only reliable counter is being more structured, more patient, and better sourced. There is no shortcut past doing the reading.
