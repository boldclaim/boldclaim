# 2 Schools Out of 30,000

The entire school Islamisation panic in Germany rests on two documented cases. Two schools, out of more than 30,000. Both incidents were corrected within days by existing state oversight. No new laws were needed. No emergency powers invoked. The system worked exactly as designed.

That should be the end of the story. It isn't, because the story was never about schools.

## What actually happened

In March 2026, a teacher at the Joseph-Beuys-Gesamtschule in Kleve, North Rhine-Westphalia, asked non-fasting students to turn away while eating during Ramadan. The context, according to Focus, was mutual provocation: fasting students were making retching noises, non-fasting students were waving food around. A teacher made a bad call in a tense moment. The Bezirksregierung Dusseldorf responded within days, stating clearly: "There is no obligation to hide or restrict eating." Done.

In March 2025, the headteacher of the Zuckmayer-Oberschule in Berlin-Neukolln invited all students to a mandatory iftar (fast-breaking meal). The Berlin education senator flagged a potential legal breach, the Senate intervened, and participation was made voluntary. Again, days.

Two incidents. Two corrections. Both by the regular chain of command (school oversight, regional government, Senate). The structures that secularists worry about losing are the very structures that resolved these cases.

## The MOTRA headline trick

When the school cases alone can't sustain the narrative, the MOTRA study gets drafted in. The headline number is genuinely alarming: 45.1% of young Muslims in Germany are "Islamism-affine." It sounds like nearly half of Muslim youth support a theocratic state.

Then you read Table 4 of the MOTRA Monitor 2024/25, which lists the eight questionnaire items behind that number. Some are serious. 23.8% consider an Islamic theocracy the best form of government. 28.6% agree that "Jews cannot be trusted." Those are real problems that deserve real attention.

But the 45% figure also includes agreement with statements like "the sexual morality of Western societies is completely depraved." 44.8% agreed with that. Morally conservative, sure. Islamist? That is a stretch. You could poll the same statement in rural Bavaria and get comparable numbers.

The study distinguishes between "manifest" and "latent" Islamism affinity for precisely this reason. Manifest (high agreement on the hard items): 11.5%. Latent (agreement mostly on the softer, morally conservative items): 33.6%. The difference between 11.5% and 45% is the difference between a genuine security concern and a front-page panic.

The MOTRA study is BKA-funded and methodologically sound. It deserves to be taken seriously, which means reading past the headline.

## The numbers nobody compares

The Verfassungsschutz (domestic intelligence) report for 2023 counts 27,200 Islamists in Germany, of whom roughly 10,000 are classified as violence-oriented. That is a real security problem that warrants monitoring and resources.

The same report counts approximately 50,000 right-wing extremists, of whom around 15,000 are violence-oriented. The Friedrich Ebert Stiftung's Mitte-Studie (2023) found that 6 to 8% of the German population holds a manifest far-right worldview. Applied to 83 million people, that is 5 to 7 million.

Both numbers are concerning. Only one gets called a "ticking time bomb."

## What "Islamisation" would actually mean

The word implies systematic implementation of Islamic rules through state institutions. That would mean schools enforcing religious observance, courts applying sharia, public life reorganised around Islamic norms. None of that is happening. The opposite is happening. In every documented case, the state reasserted secular order, quickly and without drama.

If the institutions respond to every incident by enforcing the opposite of Islamisation, when exactly is the Islamisation taking place? The answer, apparently, is in the gap between the incident and the correction. A few days of a teacher's bad judgment, amplified into weeks of national debate. Two schools out of 30,000, treated as evidence of civilisational decline.

The pattern is straightforward. A mistake happens. It becomes public. It gets corrected. That is not evidence of institutional failure. It is evidence of institutional function.
