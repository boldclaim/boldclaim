# The 10 Moves: A Field Guide to Online Manipulation

Online political discourse (especially in German-language spaces) runs on a surprisingly small number of rhetorical moves. Learn to recognise ten of them and you have seen about 90% of what any thread will throw at you.

Each move has a name, a mechanism, a real-world pattern, and a counter. Think of this as a spotter's guide. Once you can name the move, it loses most of its power.

## 1. Sippenhaft-Logik (Guilt by Association)

**What it does:** Assigns the behaviour of individuals to an entire group. One refugee commits a crime, therefore refugees are criminals. One left-wing protester throws a bottle, therefore the left is violent.

**Pattern:** "Another knife attack by a Syrian. But sure, tell me again how enriching diversity is." The individual case is presented as representative. The millions of uneventful interactions are invisible.

**Why it works on readers:** The availability heuristic. Dramatic single events are memorable. Statistical base rates are not. Ten articles about migrant crime override the reality that the vast majority of migrants never appear in any crime statistic.

**Counter-move:** Supply the base rate. "That is one case out of 3.5 million. The per-capita crime rate for this group has fallen 21% since 2014. One headline is not a pattern." Do not deny the individual case. Contextualise it.

## 2. Pro-Kopf-Trick (The Per Capita Dodge)

**What it does:** Uses rising absolute numbers to suggest an explosion in crime, while ignoring that the population being measured has grown faster than the crime numbers.

**Pattern:** "Foreign suspect numbers up 30%!" Technically true. But the foreign-resident population nearly doubled between 2014 and 2024 (8.2 million to 14.1 million). Per capita, the rate dropped from roughly 6,260 to 4,960 per 100,000. That is a 21% decline.

**Why it works on readers:** Absolute numbers feel concrete. Per-capita rates feel abstract. "30% more" is alarming. "21% fewer per person" requires a calculator.

**Counter-move:** Always convert to per capita. Show the population denominator. "The number went up because the population went up. Per person, it went down. Here is the calculation." Let readers do the maths themselves.

## 3. Torpfosten-Verschiebung (Goalpost Shifting)

**What it does:** When a claim is refuted, the definition of the claim changes. The argument migrates because it cannot hold any single position.

**Pattern:** "The media are silent about migrant crime!" You provide the Hestermann study showing migrants are mentioned 3x more than their statistical share in crime reporting. "I meant silent in public debate, because you get called racist for asking questions." The claim was about media coverage. Now it is about debate culture. The goalpost moved.

**Why it works on readers:** Most readers do not track the shift. They just see two people disagreeing. The fact that the original claim was definitively disproven gets lost in the new framing.

**Counter-move:** Pin the original claim. "You said the media are silent. The data shows they are not. Let us resolve that before we discuss something else." Quote their original words. Make the shift visible.

## 4. Topic-Hopping (Whack-a-Mole)

**What it does:** The opponent jumps from subject to subject every time a point is refuted. You disprove claim A, they pivot to claim B without acknowledging the refutation.

**Pattern:** You debunk the Spain blackout narrative. They jump to nuclear power. You address nuclear costs. They jump to heat pump expenses. No single point is ever resolved. The sheer volume of topics creates an impression of overwhelming evidence that something is wrong.

**Why it works on readers:** Volume feels like substance. A person making five different arguments looks like they have a strong case, even if each individual argument collapses under scrutiny.

**Counter-move:** Name the pattern. "I have answered three different claims now. You have responded to zero of my answers. Before we move to topic four, what is your reply on topic one?" Force resolution before allowing migration.

## 5. Kultur-Eskalation (The Culture Escalation)

**What it does:** Escalates from observable data through demographic correlation to cultural essentialism in three steps, each sounding more sophisticated than the last.

**Pattern:** Level 1: "Disgusting attitudes towards women. Muslim culture is to blame." Level 2: "When you control for demographics, culture remains as a variable." Level 3: "You are oversimplifying by using nationality. Serious research uses finer cultural metrics."

Each level sounds more academic. But the conclusion is always the same: the problem is their culture, not their circumstances.

**Why it works on readers:** The escalation mimics intellectual depth. Moving from simple claims to methodological language creates an impression of rigour.

**Counter-move:** Test the cultural variable. Syrians with secure residence status and Syrians with temporary toleration status share the same culture but show very different crime rates (KFN 2019). Same culture, different legal circumstances, different outcomes. That means the variable is not culture. It is structure.

## 6. Einzelfall-Ironie (The "Isolated Incident" Gambit)

**What it does:** Uses sarcastic references to "isolated incidents" to imply that every crime by a migrant is part of a systemic pattern that the establishment is covering up.

**Pattern:** "Another isolated incident, of course." "How many isolated incidents before we see a pattern?" The sarcasm does the argumentative work. No actual data is presented. The accumulation of individual cases, each framed with the same ironic tone, creates the feeling of an epidemic.

**Why it works on readers:** It weaponises the availability heuristic while wearing a mask of sardonic truth-telling. It feels like common sense rather than ideology.

**Counter-move:** Agree that patterns matter, then provide the actual pattern. "You are right, let us look at the pattern. Per-capita migrant crime rates have fallen 21% over the past decade. That is the pattern. Individual cases are real. But the trend line goes the other way."

## 7. Sealioning (Performative Questioning)

**What it does:** Poses an endless series of "just asking questions" that are designed not to seek answers but to exhaust the opponent and waste their time. Each question sounds reasonable in isolation. The cumulative effect is paralysis.

**Pattern:** "Can you define what you mean by far right?" "But what is your source for that specific number?" "How do you know the methodology is sound?" "But have you considered...?" The questions never end. Answers are never acknowledged. New questions replace old ones.

**Why it works on readers:** It looks like genuine curiosity. The person sealioning appears open-minded and inquisitive. The person being sealioned appears evasive or hostile for refusing to answer the twentieth question.

**Counter-move:** Answer once, comprehensively, with sources. Then name the game. "I have answered your question with sources above. You are not asking to understand. You are asking to exhaust. That is sealioning." One clear answer, then the label, then out.

## 8. Opfer-Modus (Victim Mode)

**What it does:** Reframes pushback as persecution. The person making controversial claims becomes the victim of censorship, political correctness, or mob behaviour.

**Pattern:** "So I cannot even ask questions anymore?" "Everyone who disagrees with you is a Nazi, right?" "Free speech is dead." The thread dynamic shifts from "you said something factually wrong" to "they are silencing dissent."

**Why it works on readers:** Sympathy is powerful. A person who looks persecuted for asking questions will attract support regardless of whether the questions were genuine. The emotional frame (brave individual vs. authoritarian mob) overrides the factual one.

**Counter-move:** Stay flat. No insults, no condescension, no "educate yourself." Victim mode requires your emotional escalation as fuel. Without aggression to react to, the victim performance has no stage. Keep citing facts in a neutral tone. The contrast between your calm and their claimed persecution speaks for itself.

## 9. Quellen-Dump (The Source Wall)

**What it does:** Drops three to five links at the bottom of a comment without quoting from any of them. The links do the arguing. The implicit message: "I have done the research, you have not."

**Pattern:** A paragraph of claims followed by a stack of URLs. No specific quotes. No page references. The reader is meant to be impressed by the volume of citations, not to actually click them.

**Why it works on readers:** Links look like evidence. Most people will not click them. The presence of links creates an impression of rigour that the actual content may not support.

**Counter-move:** Click one. Just one. About a third of the time, the source contradicts the claim, does not support it, or sits behind a paywall the poster has not read past. If you find a mismatch, quote the relevant passage. "Your third link actually says the opposite of your claim. Page 14, second paragraph." This is devastating precisely because it was unexpected. Alternatively, force specificity: "Five links, zero quotes. Which one makes your argument? Pick one and I will engage it."

## 10. Strohmann-Sarkasmus-Ruckzug (Strawman-to-Sarcasm Retreat)

**What it does:** Builds a strawman version of a claim nobody made, defends it aggressively, and when the strawman is exposed, retreats to "it was obviously sarcasm."

**Pattern:** "90% of white men dominate the conviction statistics!" (Nobody said this.) When challenged: "That was exaggerated sarcasm, obviously." The claim was defended as factual until it was disproven. Then it was retroactively reframed as irony.

**Why it works on readers:** The sarcasm defence is socially powerful. Challenging someone's joke makes you look humourless. The original strawman did its work (framing the debate around a claim nobody made), and the retreat to irony makes the person look clever rather than wrong.

**Counter-move:** Track the timeline. "You defended this claim for three comments. You provided numbers. You argued with people who corrected you. Now that it has been disproven, it was sarcasm all along? Defending a position until it is refuted and then claiming it was never serious is not wit. It is a retreat."

## Using the guide

You do not need to memorise all ten. In most threads, you will encounter two or three. The value is in recognition. The moment you can say "that is goalpost shifting" or "that is sealioning" (to yourself, not necessarily in the comment), you stop engaging with the content of the manipulation and start engaging with its structure.

Name the move. Counter the move. Write for the hundred silent readers who are watching the thread and have not yet decided what to believe. That is the entire game.
