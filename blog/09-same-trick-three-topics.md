# The Same Trick, Three Topics

The playbook never changes. Only the topic does.

Climate denial, Kremlin propaganda, and the German "Heiz-Hammer" campaign look like three separate debates. They aren't. They use the same structural trick: overwhelm with volume, not accuracy. Flood the zone with so many claims that the other side spends all its energy on defence. Never let a single lie get properly examined before the next one lands.

The RAND Corporation named this the "firehose of falsehood." It has four features: high volume, rapid speed, no commitment to consistency, and no commitment to reality. Once you see the pattern, you can't unsee it.

## Climate: the rotating denial carousel

Climate denial doesn't hold a position. It rotates through them. "It's not warming." "It's warming but it's natural." "It's natural but CO2 is good for plants." "Plants benefit but Germany can't fix it alone." Each claim has been individually debunked, most of them decades ago. That doesn't matter. The point is never to win one argument. The point is to ensure that anyone trying to respond is always one cycle behind.

97% of climate scientists agree on human-caused warming. 100% of national academies worldwide. Zero peer-reviewed studies refute the consensus. These facts are freely available, well documented, and completely irrelevant to the strategy. Because by the time you've cited NASA, the conversation has moved to "renewables are too expensive" (they aren't, IRENA 2025 found solar 41% cheaper than the cheapest fossil alternative). And by the time you've cited IRENA, it's shifted to "what about the Spain blackout" (caused by conventional power plants failing, not renewables, per the official Spanish investigation).

The carousel spins. The denier never has to defend any single position, because they've already moved to the next one.

## Russia: 100 contradictory explanations

The Kremlin uses a more brazen version of the same trick. When Russia invaded Ukraine, the explanations were not just wrong but mutually exclusive. NATO provoked it (there was never a written promise about eastward expansion; Gorbachev himself said so in 2014). Ukraine provoked it (Ukraine gave up its nuclear weapons in 1994 in exchange for Russia guaranteeing its borders). Bucha was staged (satellite images show bodies in the streets during Russian occupation, weeks before the withdrawal). Everyone lies anyway (which is, as it happens, the actual goal).

Putin's propaganda doesn't try to convince you that Russia is right. It tries to convince you that truth itself is impossible. If all information is equally suspect, there's no accountability. No one can be held responsible for anything, because who knows what really happened?

The firehose works because defenders play whack-a-mole. Each claim requires a sourced, careful rebuttal. The attacker just needs a sentence. "What about Iraq?" takes three seconds to type and three paragraphs to properly address. The asymmetry is the weapon.

## Heizungsgesetz: tabloid blitz as policy sabotage

The CDU's campaign against Habeck's heating law was the firehose applied to domestic legislation. The law required new heating systems to run on 65% renewable energy, with 20-year transition periods, up to 70% state subsidies, and exemptions for existing systems. It was, by any reasonable reading, moderate.

What the public heard: "Heiz-Hammer!" "Habeck wants to ban your heating!" "Forced heat pumps!" Every tabloid, every talk show, every CDU press conference. The volume was the message. By the time anyone read the actual law text, the framing was set.

The result: heat pump sales collapsed by 46% in 2024. Stiebel Eltron, a German manufacturer, went on short-time work. The industry had already retooled. The CDU's campaign didn't save anyone money. It created an investment freeze that cost jobs and locked renters into fossil fuel dependency for another generation. (Renters can't choose their heating system. Their landlord installs whatever is cheapest upfront. The running costs fall on the tenant.)

When the CDU took power, they "abolished" the heating law and replaced it with the "Gebaudemodernisierungsgesetz." Same climate targets. Same CO2 pricing. New name. The firehose had served its purpose.

## The pattern

Three topics. Same structure.

1. Flood the space with claims faster than they can be fact-checked.
2. Never commit to a single position. When one claim is debunked, move to the next.
3. Exploit the asymmetry: attacking takes seconds, defending takes paragraphs.
4. The goal is not to win the argument. The goal is to exhaust the people who care about getting it right.

The mirror field guide, a document written from the perspective of disinformation operators, puts it plainly: "We care about being persuasive. They care about being right. Those are different games."

That's the uncomfortable truth. Volume beats accuracy when the audience is scrolling. A two-line claim that feels true will outperform a six-paragraph rebuttal that is true, every time, if you're measuring by reach.

## What to do about it

Recognising the pattern is the first step. When someone cycles through three climate claims in five minutes, you're not in a debate. You're being firehosed. When the Kremlin offers four contradictory explanations for the same event, they're not confused. They're strategic. When a tabloid runs the same headline for six months before anyone reads the law, the damage is already done.

The counter isn't to refute every claim. It's to name the trick. "You've made four different arguments in ten minutes. Pick one and defend it." Pin them to a single position. The firehose only works when the water keeps flowing.
